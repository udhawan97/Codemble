## How it works

<CardGrid stagger>
  <Card title="A real map, not a metaphor" icon="star">
    Your modules become star systems, functions become planets, your entrypoint
    is Home. Every visual encodes a parsed fact — size is lines of code,
    brightness is how often it's called. Nothing is invented.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Explanations you can verify" icon="open-book">
    Click any planet: real source, a grounded explanation, and the language
    idiom it demonstrates — every claim linked to a real `file:line`. Bring
    your own Claude or OpenAI key; only selected study context goes directly
    to that provider.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Prove it, then it lights" icon="approve-check">
    Short checks generated from your code's actual structure — answers come
    from the graph, never the model. Pass them and the region lights up.
    Permanently.
  </Card>
  <Card title="A game with one score" icon="rocket">
    No XP, no streaks. A fully lit galaxy means you understand your project —
    and your star chart shows every language concept you learned on the way.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

:::note[v0.2.0 polyglot tester release]
Python, JavaScript, TypeScript, and mixed projects now share one parser-proven
galaxy, language-specific Lens notes, and an accessible language focus. The
[roadmap](/Codemble/roadmap/) stays public and the
[correctness contract](/Codemble/correctness/) is non-negotiable — a wrong
explanation is our highest-severity bug.
:::
